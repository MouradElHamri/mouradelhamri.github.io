About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate at Sorbonne Paris Nord University advised by Younès Bennani. My research seeks to build machine learning models capable of approaching the performance of humain brain in terms of flexibility to changes in environments and tasks. Armed with Optimal Transport Theory, I study methods for Domain Adaptation and Transfer Learning. Optimal transport affords powerful geometrical tools, which allow me to model tasks, compute solutions and provide theoretical guarantees of domain adaptation algorithms.

In the past, I worked on semi-supervised learning and various aspects of unsupervised learning with distributed data across multiple local sites, ranging from multi-view to collaborative clustering.

Bio

Before starting my Ph.D., I obtained a Master's degree in Computer Science from Sorbonne Paris Nord University, where I worked on optimal transport for collaborative multi-view clustering under the supervision of Younès Bennani. I also hold a Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Applied Mathematics from USMBA. During the latter, I worked on elliptic curve cryptography under the supervision of Siham Ezzouak.
