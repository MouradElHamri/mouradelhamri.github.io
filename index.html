About

I'm an Associate Professor at Paris Cité University, affiliated with the BioSTM Lab and Paris Faculty of Pharmacy. My research seeks to build machine learning models that can closely mimic the human brain's ability to adapt to changes in environments and tasks. I am particularly interested in applications related to genetics, exploring how these models can enhance our understanding of biological processes.

My research focuses on Domain Adaptation and Transfer Learning through Optimal Transport Theory, which provides powerful geometrical tools for task modeling, solution computation, and theoretical guarantees.

Prior to my current appointment, I held the role of Assistant Professor at Sorbonne Paris Nord University. Preceding this, I completed a Ph.D. and a Master's degree in Machine Learning from Sorbonne Paris Nord University. Furthermore, I hold Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Applied Mathematics from Fez Faculty of Science.
