About

I'm currently an Associate Professor at Paris Cité University, affiliated with the BioSTM Lab and Paris Faculty of Pharmacy. My research seeks to build machine learning models capable of approaching the performance of humain brain in terms of flexibility to changes in environments and tasks.

Leveraging Optimal Transport Theory, I study methods for Domain Adaptation and Transfer Learning. Optimal transport offers powerful geometrical tools that enable me to model tasks, compute solutions and provide theoretical guarantees for algorithms. In the past, I have worked on semi-supervised learning and various aspects of distributed unsupervised learning.

Before my current position, I held the role of Assistant Professor at Sorbonne Paris Nord University. Prior to that, I obtained a Ph.D. and a Master's degree in Computer Science from Sorbonne Paris Nord University. Additionally, I possess Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Applied Mathematics from Fez Faculty of Science.
